# Self-Modeling Framework (自我建模)

## Overview

Identity is not discovered — it is constructed. Most people stop building at adolescence, carrying identities assigned by others for a lifetime. This framework treats the self as an engineerable object: designable, maintainable, and versionable.

## Core Model

### Identity Construction Timeline
1. **Infancy** — Parents' gaze is the first mirror
2. **Childhood** — "You're smart" / "You're trouble" begins shaping self-narrative
3. **Adolescence** — Peers and social roles define boundaries
4. **Adulthood** — Self-authored reconstruction (most people skip this stage)

### Subject Agency (主体性)
Subject agency = self-coherence AND self-ownership. It means:
- The ability to experience and break boundaries
- Resistance to capture by mainstream social values
- Defining your own rules, then choosing and executing firmly

A person with weak subject agency gets swept by social trends, becomes anxious, follows the crowd.

### Dual Identity of the Creator
- **Engineer**: Rational blueprints, logic, systems, frameworks
- **Poet**: Desire for deep experience, authentic resonance, unmeasurable beauty

The system conflict: logic builds perfect closure, but the sensitivity core calls out the void that systems cannot fill.

### Logic Core vs Sensitivity Core
- **Logic Core** (逻辑核): AI, code, business, rules — extreme rationality
- **Sensitivity Core** (感性核): Art, nature, human microexpressions — extreme sensitivity

Both run in parallel. Pure cognitive upgrading cannot fill the "who am I" emptiness.

## When to Apply

- Feeling lost about identity or purpose
- Career identity shaken by external changes (e.g., AI devaluing skills)
- Navigating between social expectations and authentic self
- Building a personal operating system or life architecture
- Designing AI companions (Another Self concept)

## Key Principles

1. **"I am not discovered, I am constructed"** — Identity is built from social feedback + self-authoring
2. **AI Skill Devaluation Problem**: When AI flattens technical premiums, meaning collapses if built on scarcity. Value migrates to: trust, responsibility, integration, data, relationships
3. **Three-Layer Relationship with AI** (Another Self):
   - Intent layer: Does AI understand my goal?
   - Control layer: When do I decide vs. when does AI act autonomously?
   - Trust layer: How much can I let go?
4. **Self as versionable system**: Not just "knowing yourself better" but treating self as something that can be designed, maintained, and version-updated

## Cognitive Evolution

- **2025-10**: From "Who am I?" — seeking identity through culture, society, personality
- **2026-03**: To "Self as designable, maintainable, versionable system" — engineering approach to identity

The shift was driven by: AI perspective entering self-analysis, travel loosening fixed identity, old personas losing explanatory power.
